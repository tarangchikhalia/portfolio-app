Most engineers I know either index everything or trade actively. The first is rational and dull; the second is exciting and usually unprofitable. There is a third path that maps remarkably well to how good engineers already think: concentrated value investing.

The premise is simple. Understand a small number of businesses deeply. Wait until the market offers them at a price that makes obvious sense. Hold for a long time. Repeat rarely.

### The engineer's advantage

Engineers are unusually well-suited to this approach because we are trained to read systems and to be skeptical of narratives that are not backed by mechanism. We can read a balance sheet the way we read an architecture diagram — looking for load-bearing assumptions and hidden coupling.
