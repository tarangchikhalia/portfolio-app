You can read about CAP theorem for a year and learn less than you will from one weekend trying to keep your media server, photo backup, and DNS resolver alive at the same time during a power blip.

My homelab runs on modest hardware — a 3600X, 48GB of RAM, a 3060 Ti for local inference. It hosts more services than most small companies. And every single one teaches me something I would never have learned reading.

### Things you learn the hard way

You learn that DNS is the most important service you operate, and you learn this by breaking it. You learn that backups you never test do not exist. You learn that "self-hosted" is a verb that demands tense in present continuous.
